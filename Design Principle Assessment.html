Alignment — James Worthen

Vertical Alignment for header with menu and an equal size footer it starts off with a central alignment with different products going down the site until the second half of the website switches to a modular design with columns and rows to selectdifferent links in their site

Repetition — Joseph Luchs

www.google.com

Google search demonstrates repetition through a repeated pattern and color scheme. This pattern being the title in blue, the link in green, and then two lines of text in black, with sublinks underneath that in blue when applicable. This pattern is repeated (with occasional variation for I mages or other content,) no matter what your search is. There is also repetition of shape, with each result being contained within a rectangle, though the size may vary, the shape itself is consistent.

Contrast — Sarah Barrett>

www.wsj.com

The Wall Street Journal does an excellent job at creating contrast. Their entire page almost represents a real newspaper. The entire page is white, with bold, black headlines and black paragraph text. The captions are slightly different enough to differentiate from normal text to caption. Then, under all that in bright red is the "more" links to draw your attention to more articles.
